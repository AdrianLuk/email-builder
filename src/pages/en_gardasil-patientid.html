As you know, HPV doesn’t discriminate – there is no "type" of person who gets it. If you’re sexually active, you’re at risk.1 In fact, the SOGC recommends HPV vaccination for girls and women aged 9-45 years.2

Identifying Eligible Patients

Your female patient may be at risk of infection if she:

  • Is sexually active3
  • Had a change in sexual partner4
  • Had a previous abnormal Pap test5
  • Had a previous sexually transmitted infection (STI)6

Opportunities for discussion

Not sure when to have the conversation? Consider discussing vaccination against HPV to help prevent cervical cancer and/or other HPV-related diseases during visits such as*:

  • Routine checkup
  • Pap test
  • Contraception or contraception prescription renewal
  • STI check
  • Colposcopy

* Vaccination does not protect everyone, and only protects individuals against those types of HPV that are contained in the vaccine. Side effects and allergic reactions may occur. Vaccinated women should still get routine cervical cancer screening.
